// the thesis

Why I'm building this

> the problem

Frameworks became crutches

MEDDPIC. BANT. SPICED. They were built as thinking tools, but became checkbox exercises. Reps fill in fields to satisfy managers, not to understand deals. The form replaced the thinking.

> the belief

Every deal is unique

Does this person have a real problem we solve? Can they actually buy? Is there urgency? What's the cost of doing nothing? These questions matter more than any acronym.

> the approach

Intelligence layers, not integrations

We don't build connectors. We build agents that sit on top of your existing tools — Slack, Notion, Fireflies, Calendar — and surface what matters. The value is in the thinking, not the plumbing.

> the bet

AI should think, not just organize

Most sales AI just moves data between boxes. We're building agents that assess deals the way a great operator would — by asking the right questions, not filling in the right fields.

After three 0-to-$15M journeys in B2B SaaS, I've seen what actually closes deals versus what fills CRM fields. The best reps don't follow frameworks — they understand the fundamental dynamics of each deal. That's what these agents encode: not a process, but a way of thinking.
